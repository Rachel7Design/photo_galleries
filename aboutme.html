About Rachel

Rachel’s journey began behind a camera lens, where she developed a passion for storytelling and understanding complex human experiences. What started as a creative outlet—after receiving her first camera from her mom—evolved into a broader commitment to solving meaningful challenges through thoughtful, people-centered work.

She is currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Human-Computer Interaction at the University of Michigan, where she focuses on creating scalable solutions that address real-world problems. Her work is shaped by a strong interest in social impact, sustainability, and interdisciplinary collaboration, and she thrives in environments that combine analytical thinking with creative exploration.

With deep ties to Costa Rica, Rachel draws inspiration from nature and cultural diversity. Whether working with stakeholders, conducting research, or shaping strategy, she brings curiosity, adaptability, and a genuine commitment to creating lasting, human-centered impact.
